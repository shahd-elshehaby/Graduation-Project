Most Popular Pieces

A Tanouh carriage

A Tanouh carriage for the ride of His Highness Prince Farouk,
Prince of Upper Egypt, 1922-1936 AD.

A metal lantern

A metal lantern used to illuminate carriages, with the initials of Wali Said Pasha (1854-1863) engraved on its glass.

Stirrups

A pair of gilded copper stirrups.

A porcelain statue

A colored porcelain statue of a knight wearing a suit, with a wooden base—crafted in Austria.

The Alaï Private Carriage

This carriage has a rich and illustrious history, as it was a gift from the French Emperor Napoleon III and his wife, Empress Eugénie, to Khedive Ismail during the opening of the Suez Canal in 1869. The Marriott Hotel preserves a painting that depicts this carriage.

A collection of seals

A collection of seals belonging to the Royal Carriages Department.

An oil painting

An oil painting of King Fuad's favorite donkey, with one of the royal palaces visible in the background of the painting.

Caravan Carriage

A large wooden box carriage armored with zinc sheets, made in England during the era of Khedive Ismail
(1863-1879 AD), used for transporting furniture and kitchen supplies. It rests on four wheels, with a single door at the rear, a front seat for the driver, and an iron railing on top of the box. It was driven by two men and pulled by four horses.

The Kumshegi's attire

The attire of the Kumshegi, the person who would trot alongside each horse during official processions.
His duties included cleaning and protecting the carriages while in motion, clearing the path,
and holding a bamboo stick in the morning and a torch in the evening to light the way.

Activities

27of January, 2025

Today marked the launch of the first activities of the mid-year training program for 2025. The day began with welcoming the trainee students, who were divided into two groups for a guided tour to learn about the history of the museum's establishment and its most important collections, in collaboration with the Friends of the Royal Carriages Museum and under the supervision of the museum's curators. This was followed by theoretical lectures in the lecture hall, which included:
- An opening speech and a brief overview of the tasks and regulations of museum work, presented by Mr. Amin Mahmoud Al-Kahki, Director of the Royal Carriages Museum.
- A lecture titled "Volunteer Work: From Experience to Leadership," delivered by Dr. Mamdouh Farouk, Director of the Imhotep Museum in Saqqara.
- A lecture titled "The Evolution of Islamic Architecture during the Era of the Muhammad Ali Pasha Dynasty," presented by Dr. Gamal Abdel Rahim, Professor of Islamic Antiquities at Cairo University. Under the supervision of:
Mr. Amin Al-Kahki, Director of the Royal Carriages Museum.

21st of January, 2025

In implementation of the plan by the General Administration of Museum Education for People with Special Needs within the Museums Sector to train officials and heads of museum education departments for people with special needs in affiliated museums, the administration organized the first day of training on "Communication and Interaction Skills with Visually and Hearing Impaired Individuals." The training was conducted by Mrs. Hoda El-Enshasy, a clinical psychologist at Al-Nour and Al-Amal School, in collaboration with the Quality Union for Special Needs and Disabled Care Organizations. The training covered the following topics:
The Law on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (Visual and Hearing).
The importance of understanding communication and interaction skills for visually and hearing impaired individuals to avoid potential issues during their museum visits.
Methods for museum educators to attract the attention of visually and hearing impaired individuals during their museum visits.
Tools that enhance the sense of reassurance for visually and hearing impaired individuals and mechanisms for providing suitable environments with minimal available resources.
Under the supervision of:
Dr. Heba Abdel Aziz, Supervisor of the General Administration of Museum Education for People with Special Needs.
Mr. Amin Al-Kahki, Museum Director.
