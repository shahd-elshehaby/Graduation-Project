Activities

23th of August, 2023

The Museum of Islamic Art concluded the educational program "Science Cave," which aimed to simplify various branches of science and deliver them to children through practical, hands-on applications. The program lasted for three consecutive weeks. A scientific competition was organized for all children participating in the program, including both written and practical tests. The winners were honored with material prizes and certificates of appreciation in the presence of their families, creating an atmosphere of joy and happiness.

25th of August, 2023

The Museum of Islamic Art organized an art exhibition today featuring ceramic and pottery products, coinciding with the celebration of the Nile flood. The exhibition included 20 exhibitors, both male and female, who showcased their products reflecting Islamic identity and cultural heritage. The displayed works followed diverse schools and techniques in craftsmanship and designs.
