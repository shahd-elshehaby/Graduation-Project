Activities

December 15, 2021

A spectacular artistic evening featuring Maestro Nader Abbasi on the revival of musical heritage took place on December 15, 2021, at the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization in Fustat. During the evening, the artist elaborated on the significance of musical heritage and how it can be revived. He also discussed the reasons behind the growing interest in this distinguished genre of music in recent times and highlighted how various countries around the world have utilized it to lead global orchestras on stages in France, Switzerland, and other nations. Maestro Nader Abbasi expressed his appreciation for the efforts, fruitful collaboration, and full coordination between the museum's administration and those organizing such evenings, seminars, and exhibitions. These efforts have achieved significant milestones in spreading intellectual, artistic, and cultural awareness. He also referenced global recognition through events like the grand celebration of the transfer of the royal mummies to the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization and the inauguration of the Avenue of Sphinxes in Luxor.

The Ancient Egyptian Harp,
July 13, 2021

As part of the cultural and artistic program organized by the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization in Fustat, the open Roman Theater at the museum hosted a mesmerizing musical performance this evening. During the event, Egyptian harpist Manal Mohyeldin captivated all attendees with her solo performance on the harp. The concert featured a selection of beautiful pieces from the heritage of Arab music as well as new compositions by Manal Mohyeldin herself. Her performances are unique for blending classical international compositions with traditional Arab pieces, showcasing her distinctive style of musical fusion.
