The Musician's Curtain

The Musician's Curtain is a remarkable example of Coptic textile art.
It features intricate designs, including a musician and decorative motifs of warriors, dancers, and horsemen.
This piece highlights the exceptional weaving skills of the Copts, who were known for their mastery
in creating textiles primarily from linen and embellishing them with dyed woolen threads.
The curtain reflects the rich cultural and artistic heritage of Coptic Egypt.

Date: 4th or 5th century CE

Material: Linen and Wool

Discovery Location: Antinoopolis.

Activities

November 7, 2023

The Cairo International Airport Museum, Terminal 2, in cooperation with the Coptic Museum, organized a temporary art exhibition titled 'Sustainability and the Museum's Role.' The exhibition was opened today, November 7, 2023, in the presence of Ms. Jehan Atef, Director General of the Coptic Museum, and Ms. Engy Aziz, Director of the Airport Museum, Terminal 2. The exhibition includes exhibits that reflect the sustainability of heritage, especially intangible heritage, as well as tools that symbolize environmental conservation and quality of life. Among these exhibits are pieces from the educational department of the Coptic Museum, such as pottery and ceramic vessels made using Aswan clay, and jewelry pieces made of copper with galvanized wire (electricity). Additionally, the exhibition features items from the Cairo International Airport Museum, Terminal 2, including bags and tablecloths made from burlap fabric, eco-friendly paper bags, wooden spoons, wooden toys for children, as well as wickerware and heritage clothing. Informative panels depicting the sustainability of art through photography were also displayed. The exhibition at the Coptic Museum will continue for a week from its opening date.

Old Cairo, Mar Girgis, Cairo Governorate, Egypt

Today, the Coptic Museum and the Museum of Islamic Art celebrated the simultaneous beginning of the Coptic and Hijri calendars. On this occasion, a celebration titled 'Together We Celebrate... Tut and Muharram' was held. The exhibition was inaugurated by Mrs. Jehan Atef, Director General of the Coptic Museum, Mrs. Amal Sediq, Deputy Head of the Sector, Dr. Mamdouh Osman, Director of the Museum of Islamic Art, and several Christian and Islamic religious figures. The celebration included the opening of a temporary exhibition of Islamic artifacts and an art exhibition expressing Islamic art. On the sidelines of the celebration, art workshops for children and decoupage workshops were held, and a children's play titled 'Our Calendar in a Tale' was performed
