Most Popular Pieces

A gold set belonging to Princess Malak Tousson

It includes a brush, drinking cups, and a sword adorned with floral decorations and the letter "M." It dates back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty and is made of gold, found in Egypt.

The necklace belonging to Muhammad Ali Pasha

A necklace of Muhammad Ali Pasha, made up of 16 decorative units, includes 8 units inscribed with his name and 8 others shaped like gold flowers, each with a gold ring set with diamonds. It dates back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty and is made of gold, found in Egypt.

The Queen Farida Set

Is a luxurious piece made of platinum and 18-carat gold. Its crown features five graduated flowers, with the largest at the center, showcasing royal elegance. Discovered in Egypt, it reflects the opulence and craftsmanship of the era.

The golden chess set from King Farouk's collection

The golden chess set from King Farouk's collection features 32 pieces shaped like animal and human figures, adorned with 425 diamonds and colored enamel. The bases display historical figures, buildings, and Persian inscriptions in enamel. Dating back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty and discovered in Iran, this luxurious set reflects the era's opulence and intricate craftsmanship.

The set consists of nine pieces forming a desk set, housed in a wooden box

The set includes nine pieces forming a luxurious desk set, housed in a wooden box. It features three golden inkwells, a golden sponge holder, a double penholder, steel scissors, an ivory ruler, a paper knife, and a sand shaker, adorned with 166 brilliant-cut diamonds. Owned by Said Toson, this exquisite collection dates back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty and was discovered in Egypt, showcasing the era's opulence and craftsmanship.

The Princess Shwikar Set

A set made of platinum and diamonds includes a crown and earrings. It dates back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty and was found in Egypt.

The Elephant statue

This unique masterpiece, gifted to King Farouk by Mohammad Reza Pahlavi, is an ivory elephant statue adorned with a golden saddle and howdah, encrusted with pearls, sapphires, and other precious stones. The elephant features gold ornaments with pearls and gemstones, a gold chain with a bell around its neck, and ivory tusks tipped with gold. Dating back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty, this exquisite piece, discovered in Egypt, reflects the opulence and artistry of royal gifts from that era.

The collection of household items

The collection of household items at the Royal Jewelry Museum features exquisite pieces made of gold and diamonds, showcasing the luxury and craftsmanship of the Muhammad Ali Dynasty. These opulent items, discovered in Egypt, reflect the grandeur and refined taste of the royal family, highlighting their lavish lifestyle and the artistic heritage of the era.

The collection of pocket watches

Features a variety of golden watches in different designs. Some are engraved with portraits, others bear names, and some are adorned with intricate decorative patterns. These exquisite timepieces date back to the Muhammad Ali Dynasty and are made of gold, reflecting the luxury and craftsmanship of the era. Discovered in Egypt, the collection highlights the opulence and artistic heritage of the royal family.

Activities

22nd of December, 2025

The Scientific Research Department at the museum held a lecture titled 'Islamic Coins at the Greco-Roman Museum' , delivered by Dr. Salwa Abdel Mutalib, Senior Researcher at the Royal Jewelry Museum, on Sunday, December 22, 2024. The lecture covered Islamic coins starting from the emergence of Islam through the coins of the Rashidun, Umayyad, Abbasid, Almoravid, and Almohad caliphates, and the changes that occurred in Islamic currency through the ages.

30th of January, 2025

On the occasion of celebrating the International Day of Education, the General Administration of Museum Education for People with Special Needs at the Royal Jewelry Museum organized a seminar discussing the latest educational methods provided by the state for people with special needs, how they were implemented, and the extent of their benefits in serving people with special needs. The seminar was delivered by Mr. Khaled Saleh Henein, Assistant Professor at the Department of Educational Foundations, Faculty of Education, Alexandria University.
Supervised by:
Ms. Safaa Farouk, General Manager of the Royal Jewelry Museum.
Dr. Heba Abdel Aziz, Supervisor of the General Administration of Museum Education for People with Special Needs at the Museums Sector
