Report

The Avenue of Sphinxes is one of the most prominent archaeological landmarks reflecting the grandeur of ancient Egyptian civilization, symbolizing the artistic and engineering creativity of the ancient Egyptians. This remarkable path stretches approximately 2700 meters (3 kilometers) , making it one of the largest and oldest routes in the world, connecting Karnak Temple to Luxor Temple via the Mut Temple.

History of the Avenue of Sphinxes
The history of the Avenue of Sphinxes dates back more than 5000 years , when it was used as a sacred religious route for royal and divine processions in ancient Egypt. This path served as a holy trail for major celebrations such as the "Opet Festival," which was held to crown kings and renew their legitimacy as rulers of the land.

King Amenhotep III of the Eighteenth Dynasty was the first to begin work on this massive project, laying the initial foundations of the avenue. However, the majority of the current construction is attributed to King Nectanebo I of the Thirtieth Dynasty, who placed the final statues along both sides of the path.

Design and Details of the Avenue
The Avenue of Sphinxes consists of a pavement made of sandstone, with over 1200 sphinx statues lining both sides, along with other statues featuring hybrid forms such as human-headed rams and human-headed falcons, reflecting the worship of the god Khnum , the deity of fertility and water. These statues are not merely artistic decorations; they held deep religious symbolism, as the ram represented the god Amun , one of the most important deities in ancient Egypt.

Religious Importance of the Avenue
In ancient Egypt, the Avenue of Sphinxes served as a significant religious route during major celebrations. It connected Karnak Temple, a major religious center, to Luxor Temple, which functioned as a royal hub. During these processions, statues of the gods were transported from Karnak Temple to Luxor Temple, strengthening the connection between the earthly and spiritual realms.

Discovery of the Avenue of Sphinxes
In 1949 , the first traces of the Avenue of Sphinxes were discovered when the Egyptian archaeologist Zakaria Goneim found eight sphinx statues near Luxor Temple. Following this discovery, further excavations revealed more details about this important path, leading to its official reopening in 2021 after extensive exploration and restoration efforts.

Development Project of the Avenue of Sphinxes
As part of governmental efforts to develop the archaeological area, the Extension Bridge of the Avenue of Sphinxes was constructed, measuring 600 meters long and 8 meters wide , linking the eastern and western parts of Luxor. This bridge helps alleviate traffic problems and provides easy access to various archaeological sites around the avenue. The project cost approximately 30 million Egyptian pounds , and it is considered a unique artistic masterpiece as part of the national project to revitalize Luxor.

Tourism Significance of the Avenue of Sphinxes
Since its official reopening in 2021, the Avenue of Sphinxes has become a major tourist attraction, contributing significantly to the local economy by boosting cultural and historical tourism. This avenue is not only a testament to Egypt's rich history but also a national project reflecting the vision of the Egyptian state to restore the historical glory of this ancient city.

Conclusion
The Avenue of Sphinxes is more than just an archaeological site; it is a living story of the greatness of ancient Egyptian civilization, combining art, engineering, and religion. Whether you're interested in history, art, or culture, visiting the Avenue of Sphinxes will leave a lasting impression and open new doors to understanding the distant past.
